BELLE improves controllable sampling, robustness, and zero-shot performance in continuous-valued AR TTS via a new data/architecture recipe: evidential Bayesian sampling plus multi-teacher distillation. Concretely, each mel frame is modeled as a Gaussian drawn from a Normal-Inverse-Gamma (NIG) hyper-distribution; the network predicts (γ, ν, α, β) and performs hierarchical test-time sampling—Inverse-Gamma for variance, Gaussian for mean, then Gaussian for the frame—to yield principled uncertainty and tunable diversity. In parallel, multiple off-the-shelf TTS models synthesize several audios per identical text-audio prompt; these multi-source "teachers" and real recordings are combined with weighted losses to curate temporally and stylistically richer supervision. The system stacks a prenet, a decoder-only Transformer AR LM, a sampling module with an MLP denoiser, a postnet, a stop-prediction head, and a HiFi-GAN vocoder. Trained on ~72 h real + ~383 h synthetic (~455 h total), BELLE consistently beats Gaussian-sampling MELLE on WER, speaker similarity, and UTMOS in continuation and cross-sentence zero-shot settings, with further gains when scaling teachers from 3 to 7.
